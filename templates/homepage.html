Marching Band LPS
From the bright lights to the big screens.

Learn more and start today

Problem:

Marching bands require very precise positioning for their shows, however, currently the only method of reviewing positions and formations of marchers is through a band tower which is only accessible to band directors.



Solution:

Develop a system of wearable transmitters that send signals to stationary receivers that will calculate the marcher's location. This data will then be sent to this web application for both marchers and directors to review and analyze the performances of the band.

How it works.

Marcher:
As the marcher, the user is able to utilize a live feed of their position on the field as well as other marchers. The marcher is able to toggle on the members on the field based on preference. The marcher is also able to select two recordings on the recordings page to overlay and analyze.



Director:
As the director all the same options are available as for the marcher. There are some extra options as well. The director is allowed to create up to 10 different recordings that other users can analyze. The director is also allowed to change the users instrument and delete members.

ABOUT THE COMPANY

Senior design project for Texas A&M University students with the following standards upheld.

Innovative

The proposed solution should be a unique, modern, and an original way to help elevate the consistency and precision of marching band performances.

Reliable

The proposed solution should work under any conditions in which marching bands practice and should be able to provide the location of the marcher up to an uncertainty of one meter.

Design

The proposed solution should be able to be utilized for both during practice in terms of a live feed of location and after practice in terms of recordings provided by the director.

Support

The proposed solution should allow both marchers and directors to navigate and operate functions in a simple manner without the need of a manual.
